Syracuse's Non-profit Ecosystem

As a student at the Maxwell School of Citizenship and Public Affairs enrolled in the the Citizenship and Civic Engagement program, I was a part of a group researching the collaboration of nonprofit organizations in Syracuse and the greater central New York. We interviewed about 30 organizations, compiled and analyzed the data, and drew conclusions based on the results. We found that, because there are so many non-profit organizations, there is an increased competition for funding. In some cases, this leads to decreased partnership.

Inclusive Employment

I began researching what programs universities have in place for students with intellectual and developmental disabilities, but found myself more interested in access to employment post-graduation these graduates. Particularly at Syracuse University, placing students with IDD after they’ve graduated from Syracuse University has shown to be difficult. This finding is consistent with national employment rates. Despite employment incentives and training programs, the employment rates for people with and without disabilities are drastically different.

Right now, I am planning an Employment Workshop for students with Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities at Syracuse Universities. The students will create LinkedIn pages, take headshots, build resumes, and record video cover letters. Download the poster here.
